Grant Thornton Brand GuidelinesIntroductionThe Grant Thornton brand is our most valuable and visible asset. In over 135 countries, our brand brings us together. These guidelines empower our organisation by showing how our brand's core visual identity elements can be applied and implemented correctly and consistently.1. AccessibilityAccessibility is essential for a design to be successful; it allows users of diverse abilities to navigate, understand, and use our UI. Across our global CMS, we adhere to WCAG AA accessibility as a minimum.Perceivable: Code built-in to show alternative text for images.Robust: Usable for all users, regardless of needs.Simple: Clear, intuitive interfaces.2. Brand ColoursThe digital colour system is linked to the brand's colour scheme, with minor exceptions for digital presentation and legibility.Core PaletteUsed to ensure every communication is clearly Grant Thornton.Core Purple: #4F2D7FCore White: #FFFFFFDark Coral: #CF2020 (Solely used as an interactive colour for buttons and text links).Primary PaletteProvides vibrant brand recognition.Primary Dark Purple: #281440Primary Bright Purple: #A05DEF (Confirm hex in usage)Secondary PaletteUsed sparingly to add expression and warmth. Not for use in text or UI; ideal for graphs and charts.Secondary Coral: (e.g. #FF5149)Secondary Yellow: #FFC230Secondary Teal: #00B4C8NeutralsCreate soft and clean backgrounds.Dark Grey: #CCC4BDMedium Grey: #E0DCD7Light Grey: #F2F0EEBest Practice:Adhere to WCAG AA contrast ratios.Avoid blending different colour themes within the same page.Use Dark Coral only for primary interactions.3. GradientsDigital gradients are used to enhance presentation.Primary Gradient Dark Purple: #281440 to #4F2D7FPrimary Gradient Bright Purple: #4F2D7F to #A05DEFSecondary Gradient Coral: #4F2D7F to #FF5149Secondary Gradient Teal: #4F2D7F to #00B4C8Secondary Gradient Yellow: #4F2D7F to #FFC2304. TypographyFont Family: GT WalsheimThis font is clean, bold, direct, and establishes clear content hierarchy.Typographic Hierarchy (Desktop Examples)H1 (Hero Banner): 88px / 88px Line-height (Black)H2: 48px / 60px Line-height (Bold)H3: 34px / 40px Line-height (Bold)H4: 24px / 32px Line-height (Bold)Body Big: 20px / 28px Line-height (Light)Body: 16px / 24px Line-height (Regular)Button Text: 16px (Bold)Accessibility in TypeEnsure sufficient contrast (AAA for black text, AA for coloured text).Avoid using text over imagery unless it is a strong hero title.5. Motion GuidelinesMotion creates an enhanced user journey and provides validation. Our motion style is calming, inspired by natural rhythms, and characterised by eased animations.Motion PrinciplesConnected: Layered and connected to brand graphics, representing a ripple effect.Dynamic: Directional and elegant movement that feels reliable (Left to right, or bottom to top).Expressive: The mobius symbol is at the centre, expanding to further the idea of "Go Beyond".Best PracticesDuration: Simple feedback (0.5s), Screen changes (0.5s - 1s).Accessibility: Avoid parallax or motion that disorients the user.Triggers: Animation must relate to the interaction purpose (e.g., clicking a carousel).6. Global Sub-brand GuidelinesStrategy for when member firms use sub-brands.100% Grant Thornton Name & IdentityUses full GT visual identity.Adheres to global brand guidelines.Own Sub-brand Name with GT IdentityWritten name in GT Walsheim Black and Brand Purple.Descriptor: "Provided by Grant Thornton [Country]".Access to full visual identity assets.Own Sub-brand Name with Own IdentityUsed mainly for acquired entities/subsidiaries.Must use a descriptor line: "Part of Grant Thornton [Country]" or "Powered by...".Descriptor font: GT Walsheim Light (Black or Purple).Cannot use the Mobius symbol or GT visual identity elements.7. Photography & Photoshop ActionsOur photography style is Warm, Natural, and Even.CharacteristicsWarm: Positive and relatable, not cool/blue.Natural: Realistic saturation, not over-saturated.Even: Balanced light levels, avoiding high contrast or washed-out looks.Photoshop Action ScriptsAvailable scripts to correct stock photography:Too Cool / Too WarmToo Saturated / Too DesaturatedToo Dark / Too Light8. Tone of VoiceOur voice is how we make our personality heard.Three Core PrinciplesSimplify with Intelligence: We are assured. Our confidence is backed by knowledge.Do: Get to the point, keep it active.Don't: Use buzzwords or skim the surface.Approach with Warmth: We are in the business of people.Do: Lead with "We" and "You". Make it relevant.Don't: Be too casual/sloppy or overly formal.Think Clever: We are unexpected and intentional.Do: Use a smile in the mind, subtle wordplay.Don't: Use jokes, puns, or overdo it.Usage: Use "Simplify" and "Warmth" for 80% of content. Add "Think Clever" (Flair) for the remaining 20% (Headlines, CTAs).9. Social Media GuidanceTemplates are built on a 12-column grid and use 9 brand elements for consistency.Key ElementsMobius Symbol: Used as an avatar or small-scale stamp.Colour: Use core or primary palette backgrounds (Solid or Gradient).Typography: Clear hierarchy (H1 Bold/Black, H2 Light/Medium).Holding Shapes: Corner radius ratio of 1x1x1x4x (large curve on one corner).Photography: Use treatments (gradients, keylines) to own the imagery.FormatsLandscape (1920x1080)Square (1080x1080)Portrait (1080x1920)10. Application & AssetsGrids: 8-pixel grid system. 4 columns on mobile, 12 on desktop.Corner Radius: Follows 8-pixel grid (4px for buttons, 40px for banners).UI Elements:Buttons: Primary (Dark Coral), Secondary (Purple variants).Forms: Accessible text fields and high-contrast error states.Components: Accordions, Cards, Carousels, Video players.For full assets and templates, refer to the Digital Design Library.
